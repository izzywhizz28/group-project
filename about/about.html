Background Information and History


Where did the idea come from?

The Idea of Bullet Journals came from Ryder Carroll, who designed it as a way of helping him keep on track and remember things. He was diagnosed with learning difficulties as a child, and so needed different approaches towards life.

The end result was the Bullet Journal.

What is a Bullet Journal?

A Bullet Journal is a fancy diary, you can add in what you like and include lists, collections, trackers, all sorts of things to help you along with life besides a basic calendar an ordinary diary provides.

Essentially, the bullet journal is a framework for capturing your ideas. It allows you to keep track of what's happened to you and allows you to organise what is currently going on and plan for the future - Ryder Carroll.

Why should i use one?

In this age, social media is everywhere and our lives are more hectic and stressful than ever. Bullet Journals give you that step back into calm and allow you to focus your thoughts, track your health and your life and allow down time from the technological world.

Colouring books for adults are popping up all over the markets, and with a bullet journal you can take advantage of this. You can add drawings or make colouring pages in your journal as a way of destressing.
