Key Features of a Bullet Journal


Content, Layout and Format

Contents

All Bullet Journals contain the same basic pages

  1. An index page
  2. Overview pages
  3. A migration page
  4. Log pages/calendars
  5. Tracker pages
  6. Collection pages

Layout

  1. The index page should be at the very start of your Bullet Journal. The purpose of the index page is to act like a contents page, detailing where to find everything in your journal so when you want to add something to a list or fill in you're weeks page you know where to look!

    2. Your index page can also include a key, if you decide to use one, for your trackers or lists to symbolise what the tasks mean i.e. important, complete, etc...

  2. Overview pages are a great way of looking at the future in a glance.

    3. You should include an overview page at the start of each month to easily see key events happening soon such as birthdays, meetings or holidays.

  3. Migration pages are a way of carrying over leftover tasks from the previous week or month.

    4. They're a useful tool as it saves you having to rewrite out the same info at a later date as you can push the task forward and easily see what you still need to complete.

    You should include one at the end of each month, and depending on how busy you're schedule is, one at the end of each week.

  4. Log pages are simply the layout of a typical diary, you can have ones with a spread of the year, the month or the week.

    5. Ideally, if you are going to include a spread page of the year e.g a calendar of the year, you should place this near the start of your journal.

    You can include extra detail to your log pages such as what the weather was like on each day or small trackers.

  5. Tracker pages and collection pages should both be places towards the end of your journal, so as to not be in the way of you're logs and so more pages can be added in the future if needed and they'll all be found in the same location.

    6. Tracker pages and collections are essentially ways of improving your health and keeping note of things you want to do in the future.
